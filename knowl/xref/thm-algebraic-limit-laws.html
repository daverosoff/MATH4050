Theorem 116.

Suppose \((a_n)\) and \((b_n)\) are sequences that converge to \(a\) and \(b\text{,}\) respectively. Then
  1. If \(c \in \R\text{,}\) then \(\lim_{n \to \infty} (c a_n) = c a\text{.}\)

Proof.

To prove (1), let \(\eps \gt 0\) be given. If \(c = 0\) there is nothing to prove. Otherwise, since \((a_n)\) converges to \(a\text{,}\) there is \(N \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq N\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n - a} \lt \frac{\eps}{\abs{c}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Then for all \(n \geq N\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{c a_n - c a} = \abs{c} \abs{a_n - a} \lt \abs{c} \frac{\eps}{\abs{c}} = \eps, \end{equation*}
as required.
ðŸ”—
To prove (2), let \(\eps \gt 0\) be given. Since \((a_n)\) converges to \(a\text{,}\) there is \(N_1 \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq N_1\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n - a} \lt \frac{\eps}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \((b_n)\) converges to \(b\text{,}\) there is \(N_2 \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq N_2\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{b_n - b} \lt \frac{\eps}{2}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Now, let \(N = \max(N_1, N_2)\text{.}\) Then for all \(n \geq N\text{,}\)
\begin{align*} \abs{(a_n + b_n) - (a + b)} \amp = \abs{(a_n - a) + (b_n - b)}\\ \amp \leq \abs{a_n - a} + \abs{b_n - b}\\ \amp \lt \frac{\eps}{2} + \frac{\eps}{2} = \eps\text{,} \end{align*}
as required.
ðŸ”—
To prove (3), let \(\eps \gt 0\) be given. Since
\begin{equation*} a_n b_n - ab = a_n b_n - a b_n + a b_n - ab = a (b_n - b) + b_n (a_n - a)\text{,} \end{equation*}
we have
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n b_n - ab} \leq \abs{a} \abs{b_n - b} + \abs{b_n} \abs{a_n - a}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Since \((a_n)\) converges to \(a\text{,}\) it is bounded by PropositionÂ 113. Thus there is \(M \geq 0\) such that \(\abs{a_n} \leq M\) for all \(n \in \N\text{.}\) Now, there is \(N_1 \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq N_1\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{b_n - b} \lt \frac{\eps}{2M}\text{,} \end{equation*}
and there is \(N_2 \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq N_2\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n - a} \lt \frac{\eps}{2\abs{b}}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Now, let \(N = \max(N_1, N_2)\text{.}\) Then for all \(n \geq N\text{,}\)
\begin{align*} \abs{a_n b_n - ab} \amp \leq \abs{a_n} \abs{b_n - b} + \abs{b} \abs{a_n - a}\\ \amp \lt M \frac{\eps}{2M} + \abs{b} \frac{\eps}{2\abs{b}}\\ \amp \leq \eps\text{,} \end{align*}
as required.
