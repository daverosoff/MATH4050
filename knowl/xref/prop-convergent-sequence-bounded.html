Proposition 113.

Every convergent sequence is bounded.

Proof.

Suppose that \((a_n)\) is a sequence that converges to \(a \in \R\text{.}\) Let \(\eps = 1\text{.}\) Then there is \(N \in \N\) such that for all \(n \geq N\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n - a} \lt 1. \end{equation*}
This implies that for all \(n \geq N\text{,}\)
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n} \leq \abs{a_n - a + a} \leq \abs{a_n - a} + \abs{a} \lt 1 + \abs{a}\text{,} \end{equation*}
for all \(n \geq N\text{.}\) Now, let
\begin{equation*} M = \max(\abs{a_1}, \abs{a_2}, \ldots, \abs{a_{N-1}}, 1 + \abs{a}). \end{equation*}
Then \(M \geq 0\) and \(\abs{a_n} \leq M\) for all \(n \in \N\text{.}\) This shows that \((a_n)\) is bounded.
