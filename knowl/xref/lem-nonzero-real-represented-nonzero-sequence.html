Lemma 63.

If \(x \in \R\) and \(x \ne 0\text{,}\) then there is a Cauchy sequence \(a_n\) representing \(x\text{,}\) a rational number \(c \gt 0\text{,}\) and \(N \in \N\) such that
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n} \geq c \end{equation*}
for all \(n \geq N\text{.}\)

Proof.

Since \([a_n] \ne [\overline{0}]\text{,}\) there exists \(\eps \gt 0\) such that for all \(N \in \N\text{,}\) there is \(n_0 \geq N\) with \(\abs{a_n - 0} \geq \eps\text{.}\)
ðŸ”—
At the same time, because \([a_n]\) is Cauchy, there is \(M \in \N\) with \(\abs{a_m - a_n} \lt \eps/2\) for all \(m, n \geq M\text{.}\) In particular, there is \(n_0 \geq \max(M, N)\) such that \(\abs{a_{n_0}} \geq \eps\) and
\begin{equation*} \abs{a_n} = \abs{a_{n_0} + a_n - a_{n_0}} \geq \abs{a_{n_0}} - \abs{a_n - a_{n_0}} \geq \eps - \eps/2 = \eps/2 \end{equation*}
for all \(n \geq \max(M, N)\) with \(c = \eps/2 \gt 0\text{.}\)
